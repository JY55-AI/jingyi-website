about

slowly, not all at once

“I am not any one of these things. I am the space where they overlap.”

I notice light. I save sentences. I believe the best conversations happen when both people forget to perform. I am still becoming.

I studied business at a forestry university. The trees were everywhere, and so was the feeling that I was preparing for something I couldn't yet name.

For years, I worked in executive search. I spent my days listening to people describe the next version of themselves — strategy directors, investment leads, communications heads. I learned that everyone has a story they tell and another one they carry quietly.

Then I stopped. Not forever, just for a while.

I learned to make clothes. Pattern-making, cutting fabric on a table too small for ambition, sewing buttons by hand. I worked part-time in a vintage store, watching people find pieces of their past in someone else's old coat.

Now I work with drones. Tethered ones, the kind that stay connected to the ground while they rise. I handle sales and overseas expansion — building bridges between something very technical and someone who just needs to see from above.

On the side, I'm building an AI legal assistant. Not because I love law, but because I think there are places where machines should handle the language so humans can handle the meaning.
